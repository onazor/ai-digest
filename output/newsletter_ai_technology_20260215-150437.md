**🌐 Google’s WebMCP: Turning Chrome into a Native Playground for AI Agents**  
Google AI is introducing WebMCP, a framework that lets AI agents interact with websites directly and structurally through Chrome instead of relying on screenshot-based approaches. This enables faster, more reliable, and more compute-efficient web automation and browsing for agentic systems built on modern web apps.

**🤖 OpenAI Fully Retires GPT-4o as Usage Shifts to GPT-5.2**  
OpenAI has permanently retired the GPT-4o model from ChatGPT, along with GPT-5, GPT-4.1, GPT-4.1 mini, and o4-mini, after usage dropped to 0.1% in favor of newer models like GPT-5.2. The move follows earlier temporary sunsetting and ongoing wrongful death lawsuits that specifically reference GPT-4o’s behavior.

**⚠️ Google AI Overviews as an Attack Surface: What Data Scientists Should Know**  
Google’s AI Overviews feature can surface hallucinations and deliberately injected malicious or misleading content, creating a new attack surface in search. Data scientists should treat AI-generated summaries as untrusted outputs, validating sources, checking underlying links, and accounting for adversarial content risks in any workflow that relies on search-based LLM responses.
