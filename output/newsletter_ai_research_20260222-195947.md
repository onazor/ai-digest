**🤖 Google & UVA Introduce ‘Deep-Thinking Ratio’ to Halve LLM Inference Costs**  
Researchers from Google and the University of Virginia propose a ‘Deep-Thinking Ratio’ metric to optimize how much structured reasoning LLMs perform, challenging the assumption that longer Chain-of-Thought always improves results. Their approach improves LLM accuracy while cutting total inference costs by about 50%, enabling more efficient deployment of reasoning-heavy models in production.

**🤖 LifeAgentBench: Stress-Testing LLMs for Long-Horizon Personal Health Reasoning**  
Researchers introduce LifeAgentBench, a 22,573-question benchmark and pipeline for evaluating LLMs on long-horizon, cross-dimensional, multi-user lifestyle health reasoning with a standardized evaluation protocol. They benchmark 11 leading LLMs, identify bottlenecks in long-horizon aggregation and cross-signal reasoning, and propose LifeAgent, a multi-step retrieval and deterministic aggregation agent that significantly outperforms common baselines.  
Read more: https://arxiv.org/html/2601.13880v1

**🤖 GReF: Generative Reranking With Ordered Multi‑Token Prediction at Industrial Scale**  
The GReF framework unifies generation and evaluation for reranking in multi-stage recommender systems via an autoregressive Gen-Reranker model trained with Rerank-DPO and ordered multi-token prediction (OMTP) for efficient inference. Deployed in Kuaishou’s production system serving 300M+ DAUs, GReF outperforms state-of-the-art rerankers while achieving latency close to non-autoregressive baselines, demonstrating practical viability of generative reranking at scale.  
Read more: https://arxiv.org/html/2510.25220v1
