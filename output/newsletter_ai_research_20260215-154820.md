### AI Research Highlights

A quick scan of recent work and resources shaping the frontier of ML and LLM research.

**🤖 ResearchCodeBench: LLMs Still Struggle With Truly Novel ML Code (≤40% Success)**  
Stanford researchers introduce ResearchCodeBench, a benchmark of 212 coding challenges derived from 20 recent 2024–2025 ML papers to test LLMs’ ability to implement genuinely novel research ideas into executable code. Across 30+ models, the best performer (Gemini-2.5-Pro-Preview) achieves only a 37.3% success rate, with O3 (High) and O4-mini (High) also below 40%, underscoring persistent gaps in research-level code generation.
Read more: https://arxiv.org/pdf/2506.02314  

**🤖 Forecasting LLM Benchmark Scores Before You Run Them**  
Park et al. introduce PRECOG, a corpus of 2,290 task-description/performance pairs from 1,519 papers that enables text-only prediction of language model benchmark results from task and configuration descriptions. They show that reasoning-focused LLMs can reach mean absolute error down to 9.9 at high-confidence thresholds, pointing to anticipatory evaluation for difficulty estimation and resource allocation.  
![Forecasting LLM Benchmark Scores Before You Run Them](images/20260215-154820/2.png)  
Read more: https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.20645

**🤖 Evolving ML Under Drift: Unified Survey on Non-Stationary Learning, Forgetting & Adaptation**  
A new arXiv survey proposes a unified framework for evolving machine learning in non-stationary environments, covering data drift, concept drift, forgetting, and adaptation strategies. It reviews supervised, unsupervised, and semi-supervised drift detection methods plus ensemble and windowing approaches, and critiques current evaluation metrics for continual and streaming learning.  
Read more: https://arxiv.org/html/2505.17902v3
