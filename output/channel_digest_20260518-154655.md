## AI Research

_Technical papers, preprints, benchmarks, methods, and research results._

1. **Fair Outputs, Biased Internals in LLM Decisions 🛡️**
   Authors study instruction-tuned LLMs for mortgage underwriting, showing that models can appear fair at the output level while internally encoding and amplifying race-associated representations, probed via matched applications differing only in racially-associated names and cross-layer activation interventions. They find that re-injecting these latent demographic signals at critical layers can nearly flip decisions, with asymmetric effects across groups and vulnerability to adversarial prompts and lightweight fine-tuning, implying that output-only fairness audits miss exploitable internal bias and motivating dual-layer governance tests.
   _May 18, 2026 | arxiv.org_
   [Read more](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.15217)

2. **AI slide agent boosts presentation delivery quality, not just visuals 🧠**
   DeepSlide is a human-in-the-loop multi-agent system that plans time-budgeted narratives, grounds content via a content-tree retriever, and jointly generates slides and scripts with attention cues plus rehearsal support. On a new dual-scoreboard benchmark over 20 domains and audience profiles, it matches strong baselines on slide artifact quality while significantly improving delivery metrics like narrative flow, pacing precision, and slide–script synergy.
   _May 18, 2026 | arxiv.org_
   [Read more](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.15202)

3. **State-Constrained Multi-Agent Orchestration Beats GPT-4o on Routing 🧠**
   SDOF models multi-agent LLM workflows as a constrained state machine with an RLHF-trained intent router plus a state-aware dispatcher that enforces finite-state workflow stages and pre/postcondition checks for each tool call. On a real recruitment platform benchmark, its 7B router achieves 80.9% joint routing accuracy vs 48.9% for zero-shot GPT-4o and delivers 86.5% task completion while perfectly blocking all 22 injected illegal HR operations with 100% precision and 88% recall in message-level blocking.
