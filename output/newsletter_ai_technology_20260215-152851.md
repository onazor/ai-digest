# AI News Roundup (Week Ending Feb 15, 2026)

In the past week, the AI world witnessed record-breaking tech deals, cutting-edge product launches, market upheavals driven by AI breakthroughs, and pivotal regulatory moves. Below is a concise summary of the most impactful AI news, followed by detailed highlights:

### AI Technology: Scaling, Spend & Market Signals

A fast-moving week in AI infrastructure and model ecosystems, with hyperscalers doubling down even as markets start to question near-term returns. Below are the key moves shaping the technical and economic landscape data scientists will be building on.

**🤖 Mega AI Bets: Nvidia, Amazon & Microsoft Scale Up as Apple Buys Q.ai for On‑Device Intelligence**  
Nvidia, Amazon, and Microsoft are deepening their strategic and financial bets on OpenAI and Copilot-scale AI, with Amazon reportedly exploring up to a $50B investment and Microsoft reporting 15M+ paid Copilot seats. Anthropic is rolling out agentic plugins for Claude Cowork while Apple is acquiring Q.ai for about $2B to bolster on-device, multimodal AI in future hardware and wearables.

**💸 Hyperscalers to Pour $470B+ into AI Infra in 2026 — ROI Now Under the Microscope**  
Apple, Meta, Microsoft and Tesla report earnings this week amid sharply rising AI infrastructure capex, with hyperscalers Microsoft, Meta, Alphabet and Amazon expected to lift total spending from about $350 billion in 2025 to over $470 billion in 2026. As AI build-outs move from announcements to large-scale construction, investors are increasingly focused on when these investments will translate into profitable AI products and services.

**💸 Big Tech’s $380B AI CapEx Wave: What It Signals for Infra, GPUs & Cloud**  
Alphabet, Meta, Microsoft and Amazon have all raised their capital expenditure guidance, now collectively expecting to spend more than $380 billion this year (Microsoft on its FY26) to meet what they describe as virtually limitless demand for AI services. While they frame this as a long-term high-ROI infrastructure build-out, skeptics warn of an AI bubble as OpenAI alone has lined up roughly $1 trillion in AI infrastructure deals with partners like Nvidia, Oracle and Broadcom.

**🤖 OpenAI nears $100B as ChatGPT growth reaccelerates and new model launch looms**  
OpenAI CEO Sam Altman told employees that ChatGPT usage is again exceeding 10% monthly growth, reaching more than 800 million weekly users, as the company approaches a $100 billion funding valuation. To counter rising competition from Google and Anthropic, OpenAI is rolling out an updated ChatGPT model and has seen its coding product Codex grow about 50% week over week after launching the new GPT‑5.3-Codex model and a standalone app.

Big Tech stocks, led by Amazon, have shed roughly $1 trillion in market value amid growing fears that AI valuations have entered bubble territory. The sell-off is hitting major AI infrastructure and cloud players, raising questions about the durability of current AI-driven growth expectations and capex cycles.
