## Frontier Models Go Open While Infrastructure Scales Up

### What changed
The last week brought a simultaneous jump in model capability, openness, and infrastructure. Frontier‑class models from China, Meta, Google, and Anthropic are arriving alongside new chips and orchestration patterns that cut costs and enable more agentic systems.

### The bigger story
- Frontier models are proliferating: GLM‑5.2, LLaMA‑3, Gemini 2, and Mythos‑5/Fable‑5 expand access to GPT‑5.5‑class capabilities with varying openness and safety gates.
- Hardware is racing to keep pace: NVIDIA Blackwell and AWS Inferentia3 target cheaper, higher‑throughput LLM inference, directly shifting cost curves for large‑scale deployments.
- Architecture is shifting from single giants to coordinated systems: Sakana’s Fugu and Bing Chat’s long‑term memory highlight orchestration, specialization, and persistence as core design patterns.

### Why it matters
AI CoEs now have credible open and semi‑open alternatives to proprietary frontier models, but must navigate divergent safety regimes and governance. At the same time, rapidly improving inference hardware and orchestration patterns make it feasible to operationalize more capable, agentic systems within existing budgets—if teams proactively re‑evaluate their model, infra, and risk strategies.
