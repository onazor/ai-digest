### AI Research: Current Directions & Open Questions

A quick scan of recent work shaping how we do and evaluate AI research, from value choices in ML to practical advances in LLM efficiency and AI-for-SE.

**🤖 What Values Are We Really Optimizing? New Study Dissects ML Research Priorities**  
A content analysis of 100 highly cited NeurIPS and ICML papers finds that ML research overwhelmingly prioritizes performance, generalization, quantitative evidence, efficiency, building on past work, and novelty, while rarely connecting to societal needs (15%) or discussing potential harms (1%). The authors introduce and open-source a fine-grained annotation scheme and a corpus of 3,500+ annotated sentences, arguing that current value choices support power centralization and are increasingly shaped by ties to big tech and elite universities.  
Read more: https://arxiv.org/pdf/2106.15590

**🤖 Beyond Code Completion: ICML 2025 Maps the Roadblocks to Fully Automated Software Engineering**  
An ICML 2025 position paper proposes a taxonomy for AI-for-Software-Engineering tasks and evaluation measures, arguing that current generative models are still far from enabling fully automated software development. The authors identify key bottlenecks in today’s approaches and outline research directions to overcome them for both academic and industrial efforts.  
Read more: https://icml.cc/virtual/2025/poster/40141  

**🤖 Smarter, Not Longer: Google’s ‘Deep-Thinking Ratio’ Boosts LLM Accuracy & Halves Inference Cost**  
Researchers from the University of Virginia and Google propose a ‘Deep-Thinking Ratio’ metric showing that longer Chain-of-Thought does not necessarily mean better reasoning in LLMs. Their approach improves accuracy while cutting total inference costs by roughly 50%, challenging the prevailing “more tokens = better” assumption in reasoning tasks.
